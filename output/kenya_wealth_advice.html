1. The General Rule of Thumb

In the Kenyan context, to maintain your lifestyle after retirement, you should aim to save/invest 15% to 20% of your gross income starting now. Since you are 30, you have a 30-year window, which allows you to leverage the power of compound interest.

2. The "Target Retirement Fund" Calculation

A common goal is the "25x Rule." To retire comfortably, you should aim to have a nest egg that is roughly 25 times your expected annual expenses.

Example Scenario:
* Monthly Expenses: KES 50,000
* Annual Expenses: KES 600,000
* Target Fund: KES 600,000 $\times$ 25 = KES 15 Million (in today's value).

Note: Because of inflation in Kenya, KES 15 million in 30 years will buy much less than it does today. This is why we invest in assets that beat inflation.

3. Where to Put Your Monthly Savings (The Strategy)

To hit that target, you cannot simply save in a bank account. You need a diversified portfolio:

  • The Base (Low Risk): NSSF & Pension Schemes
    • Continue with your mandatory NSSF. If your employer offers a private pension scheme, contribute the maximum allowable for tax relief (PAYE reduction).
  • The Engine (Medium Risk): SACCOs
    • Join a reputable SACCO (e.g., Stima, Safaricom, Police, or Co-op).
    • Reasoning: SACCOs offer high dividends on shares (often 8-12%) and allow you to borrow 3x your savings for wealth-building assets like land.
  • The Liquidity (Medium Risk): Money Market Funds (MMF)
    • Use platforms like CIC, Sanlam, or Zimele.
    • Reasoning: Better interest than a savings account (currently 11-15% annually) and easy access if an emergency arises.
  • The Growth (High Risk): Treasury Bonds & NSE
    • Invest in Infrastructure Bonds (tax-free) or blue-chip stocks on the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE).

4. Actionable First Steps (If you don't have a plan yet)

If you are starting from zero today, try this Action Plan:

  1. Build an Emergency Fund first: Save KES 200,000 - 500,000 in an MMF before aggressive retirement investing.
  2. The 50/30/20 Rule:
    • 50% Needs (Rent, food, electricity).
    • 30% Wants (Entertainment, eating out).
    • 20% Savings/Investments (This is your retirement fund).
  3. Automate: Set up a standing order from your bank to your SACCO and MMF the day you get paid. This prevents "spending what is left."
